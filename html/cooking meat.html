Cooking Meat Properly

It is very important to cook meat properly to get rid of some infectious agent situated within the meat. Some parasites during their development have a form in the muscle of animals called bradyzoite, this is then ingested by humans when they consume animal meat and so get infected in the process.

Meat and poultry may carry E. coli, Salmonella, Campylobacter, Toxoplasmosis, Trichinella spiralis, and Listeria. Fish and seafood may carry Vibrio cholerae and hepatitis A. Thorough cooking is required to kill these disease-causing agents.

The bulk of the meat determines the amount of time required to achieve the temperature needed to kill disease-causing bacteria to a large extent. Different types of meat have different safe temperatures, because they may have different types of bacteria.

Cooking the meat properly can prevent this occurence, this is because, there is a particular temperature range at which these infectious agents can survive when subjected to heat, so proper cooking can kill them, and the meat is rendered harmless.

Tell your mum, tell your cooks, cook your meat properly. Health is wealth. Stay healthy.
